Our Research

We use multi-omic tools to understand how pediatric sarcomas — osteosarcoma and Ewing sarcoma — build immunosuppressive tumor microenvironments, and how to disrupt them.

Platform 1

Spatial Transcriptomics of OST Lung Metastases

Using 10x Visium, we profile osteosarcoma lung metastases to capture the spatial architecture of the tumor immune microenvironment. NMF-based consensus metaprogram (cMP) discovery across OST, EWS, LUAD, IPF, and normal lung cohorts allows us to identify conserved and tumor-specific programs.

Key findings include a conserved fibrotic CAF program (COL1A1/COL3A1/FBLN family) and an immunosuppressive TAM program (C1QA/C1QB/CD163/CD68) shared across sarcoma and lung disease cohorts.

Platform 2

Single-Cell RNA-seq: Ewing Sarcoma Zebrafish Model

In collaboration with the Goldkorn Lab, we analyze scRNA-seq data from a zebrafish EWS injection model. Our pipeline (Seurat v5 + Harmony + liana) resolves macrophage subclusters, trajectory states, and ligand-receptor interactions. Galectin signaling (lgals9l1→vsir) and the MIF–CD74 axis emerge as dominant macrophage-tumor communication routes.

The MIF–CD74 axis is directly connected to GoldkornLab's published CCR work and forms the central hypothesis of our active grant development.

Platform 3

Cell-Free DNA Epigenomics (FragSeq)

Our FragSeq liquid biopsy platform leverages cfDNA fragmentation patterns to infer chromatin accessibility across immune and tumor cell types. We track promoter fragmentation scores (PFE) at checkpoint loci, immune cell-of-origin signatures, and epigenetic shifts associated with relapse in Ewing sarcoma plasma.

CD74 PFE delta values now allow us to compare immune chromatin states between treatment-naive and treated patient plasma, bridging our TME findings to a non-invasive clinical readout.

Central Hypothesis

MIF–CD74 Axis as an Immunosuppressive Driver

Across platforms, MIF–CD74 signaling emerges as a central mechanism through which Ewing sarcoma establishes an immunosuppressive niche. We hypothesize that MIF-driven MAPK/ERK activation promotes tumor proliferation and VEGF-mediated angiogenesis, culminating in macrophage polarization toward an M2-like, immune-excluded state.
