Understanding the tumor microenvironment in pediatric sarcomas

We combine spatial transcriptomics, single-cell sequencing, and cell-free DNA epigenomics to decode how tumors evade immune detection — and how to reverse it.

What We Study

Research Areas

We take a multi-omic approach to understanding why pediatric sarcomas resist immune clearance and how to engineer a more permissive tumor microenvironment.

🔬

Spatial Transcriptomics

Visium-based profiling of osteosarcoma lung metastases to map immune exclusion zones and cancer-associated fibroblast programs in their spatial context.
🧬

Single-Cell RNA-seq

Zebrafish Ewing sarcoma injection models and patient-derived tumors profiled at single-cell resolution, including macrophage subtype and trajectory analysis.
🩸

cfDNA Epigenomics

FragSeq liquid biopsy platform to track immune cell-of-origin signatures, chromatin accessibility at checkpoint loci, and relapse-associated epigenetic shifts.
